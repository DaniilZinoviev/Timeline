1980s
We were involved in the research that led to the first ever cochlear implant in the UK. After nearly a decade of resesarch with the Royal Ear Hospital, the first British child received a cochlear implant in 1989.
2000
Using the results from our research, we persuaded the NHS to provide digital hearing aids as standard.
2006
The NHS rolled out the Newborn Hearing Screening Programme across the UK, based on our research.
2012
Our research let to the development of software to improve programming of hearing aids, to match a person's audiogram, and to increase the frequency range of their hearing aid. This software is now used by globally by several hearing aid manufacturers.
2019
Based partly on research we funded, NICE relaxed their criteria on who is eligible to receive a cochlear implant. This means that cochlear implants can be made available to more people who would benefit from them.
